FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions parents ask us.

What happens in a free trial lesson? +

Your child takes part in a real lesson, not a demonstration, so you can see exactly how we teach and how we build understanding step by step. We begin by helping your child feel comfortable, then move quickly into carefully chosen questions that show us how they think, what they already know, and where they may need support.

During the lesson, we explain concepts clearly, ask plenty of questions, and guide your child to work things out for themselves. This helps build both confidence and accuracy, so you can see the difference in their understanding and approach.

Parents are very welcome to sit in and observe. By the end of the session, you will have a clear sense of our teaching style, your child‚Äôs current level, and whether our approach feels like the right fit.

How are groups formed? +

Our groups are small, with a maximum of five children, so every child receives personal attention and regular interaction with the teacher. This allows us to spot gaps quickly, adjust explanations, and make sure no one is left behind.

Children are placed according to their ability, pace, and learning needs, not just their school year. This means they work alongside others at a similar level, which builds confidence, encourages participation, and creates a comfortable learning environment.

Our aim is always to find the right fit, so each child feels challenged, supported, and able to make steady progress.

Do you give homework? +

Yes. We set homework after each lesson to reinforce the key ideas that were taught, so learning continues beyond the session. Tasks are carefully chosen to practise the exact skills your child has just learned, rather than large amounts of repetitive work.

Homework comes with clear answer support, so practice stays focused, efficient, and frustration-free. This allows children to check their understanding, correct mistakes quickly, and build confidence before the next lesson.

Can we revise past topics? +

Yes. Revision is built into our teaching, so students regularly revisit earlier topics to keep their knowledge secure. We spiral back to key ideas in later lessons, helping children strengthen weaker areas and connect new learning to what they already know.

If a child needs extra support with a particular topic, we can focus on that area so they feel more confident and prepared. This approach is especially helpful before school tests, assessments, or entrance exams, as it ensures understanding is solid rather than rushed.

Future Perfect students also have each lesson stored in their drive, along with digital copies of worksheets and homework. This means they can revisit any topic at any time, even months later.

In the older years, many lessons also include videos of the exact same sessions, so students can review explanations and stay sharp in Maths.

Do you offer online lessons? +

Yes. We offer both face-to-face and online lessons, depending on what suits your family best. The same concept-driven approach is used in both formats, so the quality of teaching and level of support remain consistent.

Our online lessons are fully interactive, with live explanations, shared work, and regular questioning, just like in the classroom. Children stay engaged, participate actively, and receive the same clear guidance and personal attention.

What year groups do you teach? +

We support children from Year 2 to Year 6 in both Maths and English, helping them build strong foundations, improve confidence, and stay ahead at school.

We also offer 11+ preparation for Year 4 and Year 5 students. This includes targeted teaching in Maths, English, Verbal Reasoning (VR), and Non-Verbal Reasoning (NVR), with a focus on the skills, speed, and exam technique needed for selective school tests.
